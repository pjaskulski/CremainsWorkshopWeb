Course dates:

15th - 26th of June 2020

Applications will be dealt with on first come, first served basis. The course in each round will start if the minimal number of 5 participants is reached. If all places are filled, applicants will be offered a place on the waiting list.

If you decide to apply, contact us with the following information:

  • Name and surname, nationality and scan of the passport
  • Briefly describe your interests and experience relevant to the course, and why you would like to attend the workshop
  • Name and contact information of a referee (for applying undergraduate students)

Successful applicants will be asked to fill an application form and given details for payment of the tuition. There is a discount for early bird registration, see details below. All registered participants (with paid fee) will receive general info about course and orientation guide. Upon arrival the participants will be asked to present a health insurence proof.

Fees

Early bird fee 800 EUR is valid by the March 31st 2020

The standard fee is 1000 EUR
The fee includes:

  • 6 points ECTS ( info on ECTS ) granted by University of Warsaw
  • Laboratory costs and equipment (including gift set for microexcavations)
  • Teaching materials - a specially prepared manual on analysis of cremains
  • Administration, teaching and supervision
  • Tea/coffee and snacks in cantina
  • Certificate of Attendance

The fee does not include:
Accommodation*
Airfares and travel within the city
Personal insurance (health, travel and accidents - REQUIRED)

Deposit required within 15 days of acceptance. Payments are non-refundable.

* We can help you with obtaining the accommodation in University of Warsaw premises - see also FAQ
